HELB provides government-backed student financial support for Kenyan university students.

💼
Undergraduate Loan
Up to KSh 60,000/year for government-sponsored students. Covers tuition & upkeep. Apply immediately after getting your admission letter.
Apply at helb.co.ke
✓
Eligibility Criteria
✔ Kenyan citizen – ✔ KCSE C+ or above – ✔ Admitted to a recognised institution – ✔ Completed university registration – ✔ No prior HELB default
📘
Application Period
Portal opens June–August each year after KUCCPS placement. First-come, first-served – apply as early as possible for faster disbursement.
💳
Repayment Terms
Begins 2 years after graduation or when your salary exceeds KSh 18,000/month. Repay via M-Pesa or bank. HELB mobile app available.
💰
Bursary Top-up
Needy students can get a non-repayable bursary on top of the loan – up to KSh 40,000 extra. Upload household assessment during application.
📞
Contacts
📧 [email protected] – 📞 0711 052 000 – SMS: 0722 248 272 – HELB app on Android & iOS – Pay Bill: 200000

Complete step-by-step KUCCPS application guide after receiving your KCSE results.

1
Get Your KCSE Results
Collect your result slip from school or download from knec.ac.ke. Use EduPath AI right now to enter your grades and instantly see all eligible programmes – before the KUCCPS portal even opens.
2
Register & Pay on KUCCPS Portal
Go to students.kuccps.net ? Create account with your KCSE index number ? Pay KSh 1,500 application fee via M-Pesa (Pay Bill 290000, Account: your index number).
3
Select Up to 3 Programme Choices
Enter your preferred programmes in priority order. Use EduPath AI's ranked list to choose wisely – mix 1-2 "High Chance" courses with 1 "Moderate" for best placement odds. Write down KUCCPS programme codes.
4
Await Placement Results
KUCCPS releases results online (usually 4–8 weeks after application closes). Log into your portal account to check. You are placed in the highest-priority course you qualify for.
5
Use the Revision Window
A 2-week revision window opens after placement – you can change your choices. Many students secure better placements here. Use EduPath AI's compare feature to decide if you should revise.
6
Download Admission Letter
Download from KUCCPS portal. Report to your university on the specified date with: original KCSE certificate, birth certificate, national ID, 2 passport photos, and your admission letter.
7
Apply for HELB & Bursaries Immediately
Apply for HELB the same day you receive placement. Simultaneously apply for: CDF bursary, county bursary, and any relevant scholarships. Don't wait – early applications get faster disbursement.

The minimum for government-sponsored degree programmes is C+ (plus). Private universities may accept C plain for some programmes. Diploma programmes may accept C-. You must achieve C+ or above to be placed through KUCCPS for a degree.
Each KUCCPS cluster requires 4 specific subjects. Your cluster score = sum of grade points for those 4 subjects – 3. Grade scale: A=12, A-=11, B+=10, B=9, B-=8, C+=7, C=6, C-=5, D+=4, D=3, D-=2, E=1. Maximum raw score = 48 – 3 = 144. EduPath AI displays the raw 0–48 scale for easier comparison. Always check official scores on the KUCCPS portal.
You can apply for up to 3 programme choices in order of preference. You are placed in the highest-priority choice you qualify for. Always put your most desired course first, but ensure all 3 are realistic based on your cluster scores. Use EduPath AI's comparison feature to evaluate your options before submitting.
You have several strong options: (1) Apply for Diploma programmes via KUCCPS with lower entry requirements, (2) Retake KCSE subjects – especially the 4 cluster subjects for your desired course, (3) Enroll in bridging programmes at TVET institutions, (4) Apply to private universities that have different admission criteria. Government-sponsored (Module I): Lower fees (KSh 16,000–48,000/year), placed by KUCCPS, requires C+ minimum, HELB eligible. Highly competitive. Self-sponsored (Module II): Higher fees (KSh 60,000–200,000/year), apply directly to university, slightly lower grade requirements, some HELB eligible. Both pathways lead to the same degree with the same value to employers.
